E-Learning & Rewards Platform

The Barbados Elite Club is an e-learning and rewards platform designed to train and certify travel agents in promoting Barbados as a destination. The platform allows agents to complete interactive courses, become certified specialists, and access exclusive incentives and rewards. The project was a collaborative team effort, and I was involved from the early stages—contributing to the conceptualization, data structure planning, and core development setup using the company's in-house CMS framework.

RESEARCH

As part of the initial team, I contributed to defining the platform’s structure and technical requirements. We conducted research on e-learning UX patterns, content flow, and agent engagement strategies. This research helped us shape a streamlined user journey, course categorization logic, and a reward mechanism tailored to the goals of the Barbados Tourism team.

DESIGN

The visual experience was led by the design team, while I provided frontend input to ensure development feasibility. The layout was designed to be clean, modern, and mobile-friendly, with vibrant visuals that reflect the beauty of Barbados. My role was to align design specifications with implementation, making sure the frontend framework supported a smooth and engaging user experience.

DEVELOPMENT

I contributed to the development of the backend structure and course categorization system using the company’s internal framework. My responsibilities included setting up the database, building reusable components, and integrating reward-tracking logic into the platform. I also collaborated closely with the QA and testing teams to resolve bugs and ensure cross-browser and cross-device compatibility. The site was built with a strong focus on scalability, maintainability, and performance.

CONCEPT

The project aimed to combine education and rewards in a way that motivates travel agents while promoting Barbados as a top-tier travel destination. From planning the user journey to building the backend structure and supporting frontend execution, I was actively involved in making this vision a reality—working hand-in-hand with designers, testers, and fellow developers to deliver a polished, user-friendly platform.
